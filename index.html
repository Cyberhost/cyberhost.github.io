CyberHost: Taming Audio-driven Avatar Diffusion Model with Region Codebook Attention

Authors1,
1Bytedance

Abstract

Diffusion-based video generation technology has made significant progress, leading to a surge of works in the field of Human Body Animation. However, most field-specific works focus on same-modality driving setting, leaving cross-modality human body animation largely unexplored. In this paper, we propose CyberHost, which aims to address issues such as hand integrity, identity consistency, and natural motion under audio-driven setting. To the best of our knowledge, CyberHost it the first one-stage diffusion-based audio-driven human video generation framework, The key design of CyberHost is the Region Codebook Attention, which simultaneously learns the common topological structures and motion patterns of local regions through a spatio-temporal motion codebook, and injects appearance features extracted from cropped local images as identity descriptor. Additionally, we designed a series of human-prior-guided training strategies such as body movement map, hand clarity score, pose-aligned reference feature and local enhancement supervision to enhance the diffusion modelâ€™s modeling capabilities. Extensive experiments demonstrate that CyberHost surpasses previous works in both quantitative and qualitative aspects, while also being the first to achieve zero-shot audio-driven video generation for open-set test human images.
